Submission Requirements

Application Form Structure

All submissions must include the following components:

Submission Flow

  1. Project Identification
    • Project Title, Department/Unit, Team Members & Roles, Project Duration
  2. Problem Statement
    • Description of the public health issue and its importance (max 300 words)
  3. Methodology
    • Approach used, activities conducted, and target audience (max 400 words)
  4. Results & Impact
    • Quantitative and qualitative outcomes, number of people affected (max 400 words)
  5. Innovation & Collaboration
    • Details on innovative aspects and partners involved
  6. Sustainability & Lessons Learned
    • Long-term plan and key recommendations
  7. Supporting Evidence
    • Includes data tables, charts, photos, and reports
Important Formatting Guidelines:
  • Total submission should not exceed 8 pages (excluding supporting documents)
  • Use Arial or Times New Roman, 12pt font
  • Include page numbers
  • Submit as PDF format
  • File naming: Department_ProjectTitle_2025.pdf
  • Submit to: awards@ncdc.ly by December 5, 2025, 5:00 PM

Judging Process

Sequence of Events

  1. By Dec 5: Departments submit applications to the TCO Coordinator.
  2. Dec 8: TCO logs, anonymizes, and distributes the blind applications to the Judging Committee.
  3. Dec 8-15: Each judge scores submissions independently.
  4. Dec 16: The Judging Committee meets to discuss scores, resolve discrepancies, and reach a consensus.
  5. Dec 19: Final rankings are submitted to the Steering Committee for review and approval.
  6. Dec 22: Finalists are notified privately before the holiday break.
  7. Jan 5: A public announcement is made to all NCDC staff.
  8. Jan 22: The official Awards Ceremony takes place.

Ensuring Fairness & Transparency

  • Blind Review: Initial judging done without knowledge of which department submitted
  • Conflict of Interest: Judges recuse themselves from scoring their own department's projects
  • Multiple Judges: Each submission reviewed by at least 3 independent judges
  • Score Averaging: Final scores calculated as average of all judges (excluding outliers)
  • Documentation: All scores and deliberations documented for accountability
  • Appeals Process: Departments may submit questions (not appeals) about process to Steering Committee
